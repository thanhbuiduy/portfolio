Learning Design
Leading for Impact – RMIT (2025)
Context & requirements
  • Internal programme for knowledge workers to experiment with AI tools in everyday tasks.
  • Blend of self-paced eLearning and virtual workshops.
  • Need clear use cases, guardrails and practice space.
Learning approach
  • Scenario-based modules built in Storyline and published to SCORM.
  • Padlet wall for sharing prompts and examples.
  • Miro board to map workflows and identify “AI-able” tasks.
Result
  • 120+ participants across 3 cohorts.
  • Post-programme survey: 4.6 / 5 average satisfaction.
  • Participants reported saving 1–2 hours per week on routine tasks.

Leadership Development
Leadership Development Programme – Dai Viet Group (2021)
Context & requirements
  • Programme for mid-level leaders to strengthen coaching and feedback skills.
  • Hybrid delivery: in-person workshops plus digital follow-up.
  • Need practical tools, not just theory.
Learning approach
  • Interactive cases and branching conversations in Storyline.
  • Reflection journals hosted in Padlet for peer learning.
  • Session flows and frameworks captured in Miro.
Result
  • 80 managers participated in the first rollout.
  • 90% indicated they used at least one tool within 4 weeks.
  • Qualitative feedback highlighted “safe practice space” and “realistic scenarios”.

eLearning
Leadership Workshops – Shopee (2022)
Context & requirements
  • Self-paced course for individual contributors to strengthen problem solving.
  • Need short, modular content that fits into busy schedules.
  • Client requested data on completion and key behaviours.
Learning approach
  • Micro-lessons built in Storyline and exported as SCORM for the LMS.
  • Interactive visualisations using simple HTML/CSS/JS embedded inside modules.
  • Power BI dashboard tracking progress and quiz results.
Result
  • 85% completion rate within the first 3 months.
  • Managers reported clearer problem statements in project proposals.
  • Dashboard enabled monthly reporting to senior leadership.
